Description: "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" is a non-fiction book that provides a sweeping overview of the history of Homo sapiens, from the emergence of modern humans to the present day. Yuval Noah Harari explores key milestones in human history, including the development of agriculture, the rise of empires, the spread of religions, and the impact of technology and culture. The book delves into the social, political, and cultural forces that have shaped the course of human history, offering insights into the nature of our species and the challenges we face in the future.

Popular Line in Book:"The real difference between us and chimpanzees is the mythical glue that binds together large numbers of individuals, families, and groups. This glue has made us the masters of creation."

Genre: Non-Fiction, History, Anthropology

Publication Date: "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" was first published in Hebrew in 2011.
