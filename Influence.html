Description: "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" is a book that explores the psychology behind why people say "yes" and how individuals can use various principles of influence to persuade others. Dr. Robert Cialdini, a renowned psychologist, discusses six universal principles of influence: reciprocity, commitment and consistency, social proof, authority, liking, and scarcity. He draws upon research and real-life examples to explain how these principles are used by marketers, salespeople, and others to influence decision-making.

Popular Line in Book: "People will do anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."

Genre: Psychology, Marketing, Business, Self-Help

Publication Date: "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" was first published in 1984.
