The Silent Patient

Robert B. Cialdini

Alex Michaelides

Description: "The Silent Patient" is a psychological thriller novel that follows the story of Alicia Berenson, a successful painter who seemingly has it all. However, her life takes a dark turn when she is accused of brutally murdering her husband and then stops speaking altogether, becoming a "silent patient." The novel is narrated by Theo Faber, a criminal psychotherapist who becomes obsessed with working with Alicia to uncover the truth behind the murder and her silence. As he delves into her past and her art, he unravels a web of secrets and psychological twists.

Popular Line in Book: "Alicia Berenson's Diary, Alcestis in therapy. What could she have done to make him do this? He's a psychotherapist, he would never harm me, right?"

Genre: Psychological Thriller, Mystery, Fiction

Publication Date: "The Silent Patient" was first published on February 5, 2019.
