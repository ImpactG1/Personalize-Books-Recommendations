Predictably Irrational: The Hidden Forces That Shape Our Decisions

Description: "Predictably Irrational" is a behavioral economics book that explores the ways in which human decision-making is often irrational and influenced by cognitive biases. Dan Ariely, a professor of psychology and behavioral economics, presents a series of experiments and real-life examples to demonstrate how individuals make choices that defy traditional economic theories of rationality. The book delves into topics such as pricing, consumer behavior, decision-making, and the quirks of human psychology that impact our choices.

Popular Line in Book: "When it comes to making decisions, it seems that our rational brain and our emotional brain sometimes compete for control."

Genre: Behavioral Economics, Psychology, Non-Fiction

Publication Date: "Predictably Irrational" was first published on February 19, 2008.

ISBN: 978-0061353239.
