Thinking, Fast and Slow

Description: "Thinking, Fast and Slow" is a bestselling book that explores the two modes of thinking that drive human decision-making: System 1 (fast and intuitive) and System 2 (slow and deliberate). Daniel Kahneman, a Nobel laureate in economics, presents insights from decades of research in psychology and behavioral economics. He discusses cognitive biases, heuristics, and the ways in which our thinking processes can lead to errors in judgment and decision-making. The book offers practical insights into understanding and improving decision-making.

Popular Line in Book: "The confidence that individuals have in their beliefs depends mostly on the quality of the story they can tell about what they see, even if they see little."

Genre: Psychology, Behavioral Economics, Non-Fiction

Publication Date: "Thinking, Fast and Slow" was first published on October 25, 2011.
