The Tipping Point: How Little Things Can Make a Big Difference

Description: "The Tipping Point" is a non-fiction book that explores the phenomenon of how small changes or events can lead to large, unexpected, and often rapid transformations in society, culture, and trends. Malcolm Gladwell introduces the concept of the "tipping point," which is the moment when an idea, behavior, or trend reaches a critical mass and spreads rapidly. The book delves into various case studies and real-world examples to illustrate the factors that contribute to tipping points and how they can be influenced and understood.

Popular Line in Book: "The tipping point is that magic moment when an idea, trend, or social behavior crosses a threshold, tips, and spreads like wildfire."

Genre: Non-Fiction, Sociology, Psychology, Business

Publication Date: "The Tipping Point" was first published on January 7, 2000.
